Meet the band

DÖDAREN consists of four guys currently living in Gothenburg, Sweden.

Four pioneers

Bass guitar is played by Eric Rådegård, who often takes the center stage position during live gigs. The band is solidly backed by Jon Solheim on drums and backing vocals. Martin Claesson performs double duty as guitar player and singer of lead vocals. Lead guitar is played by Fredrik Boulund.

The different backgrounds and personalities of the band members permeate everything DÖDAREN creates. The combination of many different established styles of rock and metal forms a new style of music that must be experienced.

DÖDAREN formed in late 2010 in Gothenburg, Sweden

DÖDAREN Biography

Jon and Martin started playing music together when they were both studying in Luleå, Sweden. After moving to Gothenburg in 2010, they began looking for more people to complete their duo. Jon contacted his friend Fredrik that he got to know during their time together in military service a couple of years back. Via some other university buddies they came in contact with Eric and the band started rehearsing together as a group very early in January 2011.

Eric, Martin, Fredrik, and Jon all have history in several different constellations and genres, ranging from the softest blues to the hardest hardcore. The creativity behind DÖDAREN most likely stems from the member's different musical backgrounds, all of which contribute to the complete picture. The band is keen on creating and writing almost all of its music together in the rehearsal room, chiseling out songs in long uninterrupted jamming sessions.

The creativity of DÖDARENS quickly resulted in their first EP, Flyttar In. The EP was recorded in the rehearsal room in May 2011 and its release in August 2011 made an unexpected impression on the world wide music scene. DÖDAREN were quickly compared to large, influential bands in the margins of alternative rock, and has garnered a dedicated fanbase from all over the world.

Just as Flyttar In was completed, DÖDAREN was already half-way on putting the final touches on a set of songs that would become a new album. In December 2011 DÖDAREN took all of their instruments, amplifiers, and other equipment to a remote farm house outside Falkenberg, Sweden to record a full-length album. They set up an ad hoc recording studio in the farm house and during two weeks in the cold Swedish winter, the band recorded and produced their debut album Maen.

After post-production and finishing touches, Maen was released to the world as a digital-only album on streaming media services such as Spotify etc. Very soon thereafter DÖDAREN signed a record deal with well-known Transubstans records, who wanted to produce a digipack release. Transubstans records released Maen with a redesigned album cover in December 2012.

Since the release of Maen, DÖDAREN has been gigging around Sweden, teaming up with other bands from the Nordic music scene to meet faraway fans. Furthermore, DÖDAREN has spent hundreds of hours in the rehearsal room and several studios to polish material for the next album, due in spring 2016.
